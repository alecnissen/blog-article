What is Jiu-Jitsu?

Jiu jitsu which came from judo is a martial art that focuses on grappling, specifally ground grappling including chokes and joint-locks.

The principles of jiu jitsu are rooted in technique over strength, as well as using timing, technique, and leverage to defeat a bigger, stronger opponent.

What is a typical Jiu Jitsu class?

Class structure can vary from school to school but in short it will consist of warm-up drills, then moving on to drilling technique from specific positions, then usually finishes with live training (sparring) to put your skills to the test with resistance.

What muscles will be worked in Jiu Jitsu? Is it more cardio or strength based?

It really is a full body workout! Jiu jitsu blends both cardio, strength and muscular endurance. The muscles most frequently used in the sport are the forearms from gripping, the hip and back muscles from all the scrambling and pulling as well as the core from all the twisting and turning.

Why learn Jiu Jitsu?

Jiu Jitsu is a valuable skill to know in terms of self defense but also personal growth. It will be challening but if you can overcome the rigours and struggles that jiu jitsu brings, you are prepared for anything that life throws at you.

Also jiu jitsu can be incredibly fun and rewarding. You build strong bonds with your teammates, and classes are always fun, so time really does fly in class! It is so fun you forget how challenging it is!
